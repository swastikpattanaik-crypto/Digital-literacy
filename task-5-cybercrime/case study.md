## What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers disguise themselves as
trusted individuals or organisations — such as banks, colleges, or
government bodies — to trick victims into revealing sensitive information
like passwords, OTPs, Aadhaar numbers, or bank details.

The word "phishing" comes from "fishing" — the attacker throws out bait
and waits for someone to take it.

---

## How It Happens — Step by Step

**Step 1 — The Setup**
The attacker creates a fake website that looks exactly like a real one.
For example, a fake VIT student portal or a fake SBI Bank login page.
The design, logo, and layout are copied pixel by pixel from the original.

**Step 2 — The Bait**
The victim receives an urgent message via email or WhatsApp. It might say
something like:
*"Your scholarship disbursement is on hold. Verify your details
immediately to avoid cancellation: [fake link]"*

**Step 3 — The Click**
The victim panics because of the urgent tone and clicks the link, which
takes them to the fake website. The URL looks almost correct — for
example, "vitbhopal-portal.com" instead of the real "vitstudent.ac.in".

**Step 4 — The Trap**
The victim enters their login credentials, Aadhaar number, or UPI PIN
on the fake site, believing it to be real.

**Step 5 — The Theft**
The attacker instantly captures this information and uses it to access
the victim's bank account, email inbox, or college portal without
their knowledge.

---

## Who is Usually Targeted?

Phishing attacks most commonly target:

- First-year college students who are new to online portals and payments
- Senior citizens who are less familiar with digital systems
- Working professionals who receive a high volume of emails daily

First-year students are particularly vulnerable because they regularly
receive legitimate emails about scholarships, fee payments, hostel
allotments, and exam results — exactly the topics attackers impersonate.

---

## Consequences

- **Financial loss** — money stolen directly from bank or UPI accounts
- **Identity theft** — personal data misused to open fake accounts or loans
- **Academic disruption** — college portal access lost or misused
- **Emotional distress** — victims often feel ashamed and violated
- **Legal complications** — if stolen identity is used for illegal activity
