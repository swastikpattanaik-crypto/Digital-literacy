## 🛡️ General Online Safety

1. **Never click on links** in emails or WhatsApp messages from unknown
   senders — always go directly to the official website by typing the
   URL yourself in the browser address bar.

2. **Check the website URL carefully** before entering any personal
   information — fake sites often use slightly wrong spellings like
   "vitbhopal-portal.com" instead of the real "vitstudent.ac.in".

3. **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts
   including your college email, Gmail, Instagram, and any banking app —
   this adds a second layer of protection even if your password is stolen.

4. **Never share your OTP, password, or Aadhaar number** with anyone over
   phone, email, or chat — no real bank, college, or government body will
   ever ask for this information.

5. **Use strong and unique passwords** for each account — a strong
   password contains uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and
   special characters (example: Sw@stik#2024!).

6. **Keep your phone, apps, and browser updated** — security patches in
   updates fix known vulnerabilities that attackers actively exploit.

---

## 💳 Financial and UPI Safety

7. **Never share your UPI PIN or bank account details** over phone, email,
   WhatsApp, or any chat platform — your bank will NEVER ask for this.
   If someone claims to be from your bank and asks for it, hang up
   immediately and call your bank's official helpline.

8. **Be extremely careful with "collect money" or "receive payment"
   requests** on Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm — scammers send fake
   payment requests that actually DEDUCT money from your account when
   you enter your PIN. Receiving money never requires your PIN.

---

## 📱 Safe Digital Habits

9. **Do not use public Wi-Fi** (at cafes, bus stands, or railway stations)
   to log into banking apps, college portals, or any account with
   sensitive information — public networks can be monitored by attackers.

10. **Log out of shared or college computer systems** after every session —
    never save passwords on devices that are not your own.

---

## 📢 How to Report Cybercrime in India

If you suspect you have been targeted or fallen victim to any cybercrime:

- 🌐 **National Cyber Crime Portal:** https://cybercrime.gov.in
- 📞 **Cyber Crime Helpline:** 1930 *(available 24x7, free to call)*

**Report immediately** — the sooner you report, the higher the chance
of recovering lost funds or preventing further damage.
